Reversing Time

The process of slowing down/reversing time

The physical world

This image simulates how the physical world would look if it reversed. Things like vehicles could move backwards frantically, becoming blurs that are no longer material but have now become swallowed by time.

The physical world

This image demonstrates how the physical world would look if it became a blur; we are open to our own interpretation. People begin to fade into the background; becoming a motion blur that has been submerged by time.

How time is perceived

This image creates an interesting blur of movement; we are not fixated on one thing; we are open to our own interpretation, we may start to think about which way the subject is moving, how quickly it is moving, how long the shutter was open for etc.

Movement of time

When portraying time through slow shutter speed, it often seems as if time is passing through movement, it allows us to see as much movement as possible captured in a few seconds.

This particular photo portrays a story of emotion; even though there is blurred movement we still see defined facial expressions.

We are left open to our own interpretation of what this photo is trying to portray.

Distortion of time

These mixed media pieces explore the idea of time reversing, glitching and swallowing the physical world around us. Through the scanning, layering and editing of tactile textures, these pieces show the deterioration and distortion that would occur as time itself collapsed into chaos.

Telling a story

These images of the sea taken with a slow shutter speed; tell a story of how peaceful the sea seems when it is a motion blur, the different shades of blue create a calm atmosphere, where you feel like you are losing focus; time stops, putting your mind at rest. The horizon of these three images line up, almost telling a story through separate images, that form as a whole.
